Dental Appointment Scheduling Procedure

Like any other skill, dental appointment scheduling requires time and practice to get right. So, the first step is to understand proper scheduling procedures.

Steps for Dental Appointment Scheduling

Here are the general guidelines you should follow when performing dental appointment scheduling tasks:

    1. Split the process into two blocks of time. Schedule morning appointments from noon backward. On the other hand, you should try to schedule afternoon
    appointments from noon onwards. This configuration could help you maintain maximum productivity while ensuring that most of the day is scheduled for patients.
    In addition, coordinate with your colleagues to use empty time slots for overflow, staff meetings, or last-minute appointments.

    2. Prioritize appointments. In healthcare, patient visits usually vary in the level of care that’s requ br ired and the time that’s needed. Remember to consider
    these factors when you schedule appointments. Although some patients may require same-day appointments, you can settle other patient issues with a phone call
    or email.

    3. Send appointment reminders through text and email. You can ensure a greater number of kept appointments and on-time arrivals with an appointment reminder
    software system. No-shows are inconvenient and costly, especially if the dentist is busy and has a waitlist. Everyone misses out when there’s a no-show.

    4. Create a patient wait list. When it comes to late cancellations, a patient waitlist is your best friend. Why? It can help you make the best of this lost time.
    It doesn’t have to be “lost time” at all. Use a patient scheduling platform that includes a waitlist function. Then, you can send a notification if an open slot
    becomes available and try to get it booked. This will help save the practice time and revenue.

    5. Use an automated patient recall system. Although you must check your practice’s regulations first, putting a computerized recall system in place can be valuable.
    This system would help ensure that patients arrive on time for their scheduled appointments. It can also bring back patients who haven’t seen this dentist in quite
    some time. Sometimes all it may take is a quick reminder email, text, or voicemail.
